Case Study 1 - WALMART

Walmart is one of the largest retailers in the world, and the company has invested heavily in AI to improve its ecommerce operations. Walmart uses machine learning algorithms to optimize pricing, improve search capabilities, and personalize the shopping experience for its customers. For example, Walmart's AI algorithms analyze customer data to determine which products are most likely to sell at certain price points, allowing the company to adjust pricing in real-time to maximize revenue. Walmart has also implemented AI-powered chatbots to improve customer service, allowing shoppers to get instant assistance with their purchases.
‍
Lauren Desegur, VP of customer experience engineering at WalmartLabs said, “We’re essentially creating a bridge where we are enhancing the shopping experience through machine learning. We want to make sure there is a seamless experience between what customers do online and what they do in our stores.”While its arch nemesis in business may be Amazon.com, Walmart has the advantage of using the best of both worlds—with over 11,000 brick-and-mortar stores and its online experience—in its laboratory to develop retail tech that catapults sales and customer satisfaction. Walmart was an early adopter of RFID to track inventory and has a tech incubator called Store No. 8 in Silicon Valley to“incubate, invest in, and work with other startups, venture capitalists and academics to develop its own proprietary robotics, virtual and augmented reality, machine learning and artificial intelligence technology.”

Case Study 2 - Stich Fix

Stitch Fix is a personal styling service that has used AI to improve its recommendations and increase customer satisfaction. The company uses machine learning algorithms to analyze customer data and create personalized clothing recommendations for each customer. Stitch Fix's algorithms analyze factors such as the customer's style preferences, body type, and purchase history to determine which items are most likely to be a good fit. The company also uses natural language processing (NLP) technology to analyze customer feedback and improve the accuracy of its recommendations.

According to a report by Forbes, Stitch Fix's use of AI has helped the company achieve a 30% increase in revenue and a 22% increase in customer satisfaction.

Case Study 3 - Sephora

Sephora is a beauty retailer that has used AI to create a personalized shopping experience for its customers. The company's mobile app uses computer vision technology to allow customers to "try on" makeup virtually, and the app's AI-powered recommendation engine suggests products based on the customer's skin tone, preferences, and past purchases. Sephora also uses AI to optimize its supply chain operations and reduce waste, allowing the company to offer a wider range of products without increasing its inventory costs.

In store, Sephora has been able to create a highly curated experience for its consumer by finding ways for the consumer to interact with AI as they would a traditional in-store sales rep. In the short term, Sephora is investing in AI and partnering with personalization specialists such as Dynamic Yield to provide personalized product recommendations to customers.
